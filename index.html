A Real Mind-Blender

So, I've basically just started dabbling in a couple different design areas. First, I tried my hand at designing a simple video-game in Unity, and while that was pretty good fun...I kind of lost steam at that point because I wasn't too sure how to progress. Second, I decided to make this website! I'm pretty happy with it so far, even though I know it's fairly amateur-looking. I know I can just keep improving it over time, and tinkering with it - so I'm content to let it tick over for now.

The final thing I started working on this weekend is something called Blender. Many of you have probably heard of it already, but just in case, I have included a link to the website below. Blender is essentially, as far as I understand it, an open-source piece of software...which is really pretty powerful. I'm a video-editor in my spare time, and have been interested in digital design since I started using a computer, so the possibilities that Blender holds excite me quite a bit. After...well, most of the day today to be honest, I finally had something I was happy to post here: my treasure chest!

Again, it's basic, it's amateur, and I used a tutorial to guide me along, but I'm hoping it's the first in a series of things I can put together to further my understanding of the program. If any of you want to check out Blender, I would highly reccommend it - even for people who have never tackled 3D modelling before. Just follow this link to download the program, and then check out YouTube to find amazing tutorials from some awesome people. Until next time!
